# Coping

The coping on a skateboard ramp is the metal tube that sits at the intersection
of the deck and the curve of the ramp. It's purpose is to provide a solid lip
that skateboard wheels and trucks can traverse without damaging the ramp. The
coping is positioned by a notch in the ribs of the ramp.

Some care needs to be taken when calculating the dimensions of the notch that
the coping fits into. If the notch is too shallow vertically, the coping sits
too high above the deck, the skateboard can get hung up, causing the rider to
fall. If the notch is too deep, the skateboard can contact the deck, causing the
rider to fall or damaging the deck. If the notch is not deep enough
horizontally, the coping protrudes too far over the ramp causing a bump that the
skateboard may not be able to cross, again throwing the rider off. If the coping
is inset too far because the notch is too deep horizontally, the rider may not
feel the top of the deck and could injure themselves.

# Dimensions of the coping

The pipe that will be used for the coping will be:

- 50.8mm inside diameter
- 60.3mm outside diameter
- it should be the entire width of the ramp

# Dimensions of the notch

The notch is cut in the ribs of the ramp, at the intersection of the top deck
and the curve of the rib.

The notch should be cut such that the coping, when put in place:
- protrudes 3.2mm over the ramp; that is, in the horizontal direction.
- protrudes 6.4mm above the top deck; that is, in the vertical direction.
